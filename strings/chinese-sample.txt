<p><b>Wang Hsing-ching</b> (王杏慶/王杏庆 Wáng Xìngqìng) (born in <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1946" title="1946">1946</a>), who has a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pseudonym" title="Pseudonym">pseudonym</a> of <b>Nanfang Shuo</b> (南方朔 Nán Fāngshuò), is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journalist" title="Journalist">journalist</a>, political <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commentator" title="Commentator">commentator</a>, and cultural <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Critic" title="Critic">critic</a>. Today, he is the chief editor and writer of <a href="http://www.new7.com.tw/" class="external text" title="http://www.new7.com.tw/" rel="nofollow"><i>The Journalist</i> magazine</a>
(《新新聞週刊》), with commentaries on current issues in major newspapers. His
writings, including Western ideas analysis, social phenomena criticism,
and literature comments, are all regarded as very influential.
Crediting his intellectual contribution to the society, he is known as
"the most industrious private scholar in Taiwan".</p>

<p><a name="Background" id="Background"></a></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wang_Hsing-ching&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline">Background</span></h2>
<p><b>Wang Hsing-ching</b> earned his undergraduate degree in the <a href="http://www.fo.ntu.edu.tw/fo_e/vision_e.htm" class="external text" title="http://www.fo.ntu.edu.tw/fo_e/vision_e.htm" rel="nofollow">School for Forestry and Resource Conservation</a>, from <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Taiwan_University" title="National Taiwan University">National Taiwan University</a> (臺大森林系) and a doctorate degree in <a href="http://www.pccu.edu.tw/" class="external text" title="http://www.pccu.edu.tw/" rel="nofollow">Chinese Culture University</a>(文化大學實業計畫研究所博士班). <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diaoyutai" class="mw-redirect" title="Diaoyutai">The Diaoyutai Island (Senkaku Islands)</a>
incident (釣魚台事件) made Wang reach an ephiphany to become a journalist.
During that period, Wang abandoned his overseas study plan and stayed
in Taiwan, starting his career. In the 1970s, Wang was a reporter for
the <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan_Times" title="Taiwan Times">Taiwan Times</a>,</i> and afterwards worked as a writer, vice general editor, and general editor in <i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_China_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="The China Times">The China Times</a></i>
(《中國時報》). Although he never received technical university training in
journalism, he has more than twenty years of experience in the mass
media as a news commentator.</p>
<p><a name="Style_and_Major_Themes_of_Writing" id="Style_and_Major_Themes_of_Writing"></a></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wang_Hsing-ching&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Style and Major Themes of Writing">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline">Style and Major Themes of Writing</span></h2>
<p>Wang, as an author, began with topics mainly concerned about
cultural changes, political changes, and the controversies in society.
His early works are based on the themes of describing and criticizing
cultures and politics, e.g. <i>Di guo shu yi yu Taiwan du li yun dong</i>
(《帝國主義與臺灣獨立運動》). Later in his career, he started publishing critical
book reviews . Recently, Wang has published a series of books about
"Language". <i>Magical Eyes,</i> his latest book, (《魔幻之眼》) is his
first published work in the book commentary collection. As a tutor
through writing, his analysis focuses on culture, history, thoughts,
politics, etc.</p>
<p><a name="Contribution_to_Journalism" id="Contribution_to_Journalism"></a></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wang_Hsing-ching&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Contribution to Journalism">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline">Contribution to Journalism</span></h2>
<p>Wang has never been shy expressing his critical opinions on current
affairs. In the summer of 2004, he attended a forum in the Hong Kong
Convention and Exhibition Center concerning the political issues in
Taiwan and the relation across the <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan_Strait" title="Taiwan Strait">Taiwan Strait</a>
after the Presidential election. He suggested that the two shores of
the Strait should make positive interactions in order to solve the
rational problems.</p>
<p>As a journalist and the chief writer of <a href="http://www.new7.com.tw/" class="external text" title="http://www.new7.com.tw/" rel="nofollow"><i>The Journalist magazine</i></a>,
he has his own independent analysis towards the political issues. Not
long after President Chen Shuibien gave the speech of “One State on
Each Side”, he wrote critics on it, which was exactly to the point,
“democracy in Taiwan is already moving towards a new and populist type
of soft politics of terror”.</p>
<p><a name="Views_on_Journalism" id="Views_on_Journalism"></a></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wang_Hsing-ching&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Views on Journalism">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline">Views on Journalism</span></h2>
<p>In a public discussion titled “How to build a mass media culture
with “taste”? Wang criticized the current mass media culture. He
pointed out that if the speakers/interviewees could not state their
stance clearly, they had to “pay the price” in the sense that reporters
might make twist their words to make a story more dramatic. The
unethical reporting of some journalists disappointed Wang, and he urged
that journalists had to realize that it was shameful to have
disregarded the obligation to report the truth.</p>
<p>At societal level, he emphasized that not only does the mass media
deliver information to the public, but it also affects the social
structure and the political environment. He noted that journalists
should bear in mind that they have great responsibilities to the
general public.</p>
<p>Particularly in Taiwan, he put the blame of the current situation on
changes in political conditions. During this unstable period, different
types of mass media emerged. As advertising plays a greater role in the
mass media, it is inevitable that politicians would make use of the
mass media for propaganda. However, he said that there are still
journalists who stand by their principles, although it remains
difficult for them to uphold the journalistic ethics.</p>
<p><a name="Seeing_Literature_as_Journalism" id="Seeing_Literature_as_Journalism"></a></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wang_Hsing-ching&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Seeing Literature as Journalism">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline">Seeing Literature as Journalism</span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width: 242px;"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Image:NanRTHK.jpg" class="image" title="Nanfang Shuo in an RTHK interview"><img alt="Nanfang Shuo in an RTHK interview" src="chinese-sample_files/NanRTHK.jpg" class="thumbimage" border="0" height="180" width="240"></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">Nanfang Shuo in an <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RTHK" class="mw-redirect" title="RTHK">RTHK</a> interview</div>
</div>
</div>
<p>In an RTHK TV program "Merry Book Worm" in October 2003, (<a href="http://www.rthk.org.hk/rthk/tv/bookworm/20031026.html" class="external text" title="http://www.rthk.org.hk/rthk/tv/bookworm/20031026.html" rel="nofollow">愈讀愈快樂</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span title="The preceding link appears to be dead&nbsp;since April 2007" style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Dead_external_links" title="Wikipedia:Dead external links">dead link</a> – <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/*/http://www.rthk.org.hk/rthk/tv/bookworm/20031026.html" class="external text" title="http://web.archive.org/web/*/http://www.rthk.org.hk/rthk/tv/bookworm/20031026.html" rel="nofollow">history</a></i>]</span></sup>,
5 Oct 04.) Wang shared his opinions on how to choose suitable books for
ourselves. He raised questions on how topics of certain discourses are
set, why the topics are written the way they are, and how the written
formats differ from author to author, and suggested that these are the
aspects we have to think about when we choose books to read. Also, Wang
stressed that it is necessary for every author to get the basic and
wide-ranging knowledge on different areas before having any disscusion
or analysis. With his literature commentaries in <i>Magical Eyes</i> (《魔幻之眼》), Nanfang Shuo attempts to give his readers a brief overview on his recommended books to help them choose readings.</p>
<p>Wang’s views on literature ties in well in the principles in
journalism. While literature commenting allows his audience to have a
knowledge of what is currently happening in the world, reporters are
also doing the same thing: making readers be aware of current affairs.
Speaking about journalism, he said that we, as readers, have to be
careful about different reporters’ stances and their different
perspectives based on various sources. On the other hand, reporters
should always bear the obligation to the truth and be objective by
relying on the more reliable sources.</p>
<p>In another interview from <a href="http://news.pchome.com.tw/life/cna/20030408/index-20030408165736010244.html" class="external text" title="http://news.pchome.com.tw/life/cna/20030408/index-20030408165736010244.html" rel="nofollow">Central News Agency (中央社)</a><sup class="noprint Inline-Template"><span title="The preceding link appears to be dead&nbsp;since April 2007" style="white-space: nowrap;">[<i><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Dead_external_links" title="Wikipedia:Dead external links">dead link</a> – <a href="http://web.archive.org/web/*/http://news.pchome.com.tw/life/cna/20030408/index-20030408165736010244.html" class="external text" title="http://web.archive.org/web/*/http://news.pchome.com.tw/life/cna/20030408/index-20030408165736010244.html" rel="nofollow">history</a></i>]</span></sup>
in Taiwan, Wang pointed out that readers are not encouraged to spend
too much time on books which are too difficult to be digested or too
easy to read. In the interview, Wang encouraged readers to find the
books which would motivate them and those which can give them new
perspectives. Applying Wang's views on reading, journalism, and news
reports, readers should focus their attention on news of particular
interest to them, ones which they feel comfortable to read and
criticize. Moreover, Wang believes that readers are responsible for the
verification of reports.</p>
<p><a name="Views_on_Reading" id="Views_on_Reading"></a></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wang_Hsing-ching&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Views on Reading">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline">Views on Reading</span></h2>
<p>As a commentator of literature, Wang welcomes all different genres
of books. He firmly believes that all books can be beneficial and that
the more one reads, the more one will gain in that field of expertise.
He recalled that in his childhood, he was unable to do anything but
read.</p>
<p>One belief of Wang is that wisdom in different aspects are all
inter-related. For him, the concepts of literature, history, and social
theory can be easily understood. He believes that literature, poetry,
politics, and philosophy are mutually related. Having this
understanding, Wang likes to use poems to interpret international
politics and the economic phenomenon in recent years. This proves that
as a commentator of current affairs and as chief writer of <a href="http://www.new7.com.tw/" class="external text" title="http://www.new7.com.tw/" rel="nofollow"><i>The Journalist</i></a>, he uses literary style to write journalistic articles.</p>
<p>A few years ago, Wang discovered an interest in linguistics. Some of
the inspirations of Wang stems from the Chinese classic literature,
novels, and prose, while some of them comes from news reports.
Researching the origin and history of the common vocabularies, he also
investigated the changing process of these vocabularies in society. To
him, this changing process can be seen from the use of language in news
reports, for the language the reporters used marks the changes in
social value. In fact, his motivation and his way to learn about the
origins of vocabulary is very applicable to the elements of journalism:
both fields require accurate information, and verification by different
reliable sources. We can only tell the truth (of events or the origin
of vocabularies) after verifications.</p>
<p>Wang is a <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libertarian" class="mw-redirect" title="Libertarian">libertarian</a>,
and he believes that it is important to be aware of the language
ambiguity in understanding readings. As there are some writers who
easily confuse readers, with or without intention, great language
skills and a critical mind are essential to make yourself clear about
what you are reading and what the writers are telling.</p>
<p><a name="Comments_on_literatures" id="Comments_on_literatures"></a></p>
<h2><span class="editsection">[<a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Wang_Hsing-ching&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Comments on literatures">edit</a>]</span> <span class="mw-headline">Comments on literatures</span></h2>
<p>Wang was one of the committee members to adjudicate the “20th
century 100 greatest Chinese novels”. As the novels revealed to Wang a
feeling of sorrow and a sense of pressure, he commented the 20th
century Chinese novels as a reflection of Chinese history in the past
100 years which he saw a difficult and suffering period for the
intellectuals.</p>
